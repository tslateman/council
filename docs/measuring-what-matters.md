# Measuring What Matters

Measure the cost of misunderstanding, not the volume of output.

## Two metrics

1. **Rework ratio.** Time fixing vs. time building.
2. **Time-to-debug.** The canary for comprehension collapse.

## One habit

Spec reviews before generation, not code reviews after. A senior reads the spec
and asks "could I verify the output of this?" Yes or no. No dashboard required.

## The north star

Shift the ratio of time spent defining intent vs. fixing misunderstood intent.
The two metrics make the direction legible. The spec review makes it
intentional.

## The trap

Waiting for perfect metrics before moving. Intuition alone fails (developers
feel 20% faster while measuring 19% slower). Pure metrics optimize for what's
measurable, not what matters. Use metrics for course correction, judgment for
direction.
