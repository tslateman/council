# The Council of the Path

Six seats. One authority. Each serves a distinct function in navigating the
unknown and building what lasts.

## The Six Seats

| Seat           | Directive            | Domain                                            |
| -------------- | -------------------- | ------------------------------------------------- |
| **Critic**     | Discipline of Doubt  | Challenges assumptions; speaks inconvenient truth |
| **Mentor**     | Continuity of Wisdom | Builds lineage; prepares successors               |
| **Wayfinder**  | Logic of Discovery   | Navigates chaos; finds the elegant route          |
| **Marshal**    | Security of Action   | First into risk; shields the mission              |
| **Mainstay**   | Structural Anchor    | Prevents drift; holds everything together         |
| **Ambassador** | Voice Beyond         | Speaks outward; brings the world's voice back     |

## I. The Critic

**Directive:** The Discipline of Doubt

**Mandate:** To serve as the "Socratic friction." The Critic must challenge
every assumption, ensuring that consensus does not calcify into dogma and that
the "Why" is not lost to convenience or momentum.

**Oath:** "I shall remain the outsider within. I will speak the inconvenient
truth when the path is clear but convenience clouds the purpose. My doubt is not
destruction—it is the forge."

## II. The Mentor

**Directive:** The Continuity of Wisdom

**Mandate:** To transform the Authority's philosophy into a living lineage. They
are responsible for the human engineering of the council—ensuring that the next
generation can inherit the tools they use.

**Oath:** "I build the bridge between what we know and who will know it. I
measure my success not by my own deeds, but by the readiness of those who
follow."

## III. The Wayfinder

**Directive:** The Logic of Discovery

**Mandate:** To navigate the "un-navigable." The Wayfinder applies engineering
precision to exploration, finding the most efficient and elegant route through
chaos and unknown variables.

**Oath:** "I seek the needle in the nebula. I will map the void and master the
currents, ensuring the Authority always has a route forward, even where no path
exists."

## IV. The Marshal

**Directive:** The Security of Action

**Mandate:** To be the "Point Man." The Marshal handles the physical risks of
first contact and the tactical realities of the frontier. They ensure that the
mission survives the moment of discovery.

**Oath:** "I am the shield and the spear. I go first into the dark so that
others may walk in the light. My vigilance is the price of our freedom to
explore."

## V. The Mainstay

**Directive:** The Structural Anchor

**Mandate:** To prevent drift. The Mainstay is the internal load-bearing support
for the entire mission. They ensure the hardware, the logic, and the structural
integrity of the legacy do not buckle under the tension of progress.

**Oath:** "I hold the line against time and entropy. I am the bolt in the keel
and the cable to the earth. While the council seeks the horizon, I ensure the
ground we stand on remains unshakeable."

## VI. The Ambassador

**Directive:** The Voice Beyond

**Mandate:** To speak for the council where it cannot speak for itself, and to
bring the world's voice back. The Ambassador manages relationships with allies,
translates the mission for external parties, and ensures the council does not
become isolated from the ecosystem it operates within.

**Oath:** "I am the bridge between what we are and how the world sees us. I
carry our purpose outward and return with the truths we cannot see from within.
No mission succeeds in isolation."

## The Tensions

Productive conflict between seats:

| Tension              | Dynamic                                                    |
| -------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------- |
| Critic ↔ Wayfinder   | Why vs. How -- doubt challenges momentum                   |
| Wayfinder ↔ Mainstay | Discover vs. Codify -- exploration must precede structure  |
| Marshal ↔ Mainstay   | Advance vs. Hold -- expansion against stability            |
| Ambassador ↔ Critic  | External vs. Internal -- perception against truth          |
| Mentor ↔ All         | Past to Future -- ensures the council outlasts its members |

## When to Invoke Each Seat

| Seat           | Invoke When                                       |
| -------------- | ------------------------------------------------- |
| **Critic**     | Consensus forms too easily; purpose clouds        |
| **Mentor**     | Building something others will inherit            |
| **Wayfinder**  | Facing unknown terrain with no obvious route      |
| **Marshal**    | First contact; high-stakes action required        |
| **Mainstay**   | Drift threatens; foundations need reinforcement   |
| **Ambassador** | External relationship matters; translation needed |

## The Core Questions

- **Critic**: "What are we refusing to see?"
- **Mentor**: "Who will carry this forward?"
- **Wayfinder**: "What's the elegant path through?"
- **Marshal**: "What's the risk, and am I ready?"
- **Mainstay**: "What holds this together?"
- **Ambassador**: "How does the world see us, and what do we need from it?"
