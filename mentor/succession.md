# Building Succession

Measure your success by the readiness of those who follow.

## The Mentor's Inversion

Everything in [career advancement](../critic/career.md) has a mirror. The Critic
asks "How do I grow?" The Mentor asks "How do I grow others?"

| Critic Frame                   | Mentor Frame                     |
| ------------------------------ | -------------------------------- |
| Get sponsors                   | Be a sponsor                     |
| Make my work visible           | Make their work visible          |
| Force Multiplier as tactic     | Force Multiplier as practice     |
| Find opportunities             | Create opportunities for others  |
| Build my reputation            | Build their reputation           |
| Demonstrate Staff-level impact | Cultivate Staff-level capability |

**The test:** If you left tomorrow, would someone be ready?

## The Eight Concepts, Inverted

| Concept               | Self-Oriented (Critic)              | Other-Oriented (Mentor)              |
| --------------------- | ----------------------------------- | ------------------------------------ |
| **Processing Power**  | Solve hard problems quickly         | Teach problem decomposition          |
| **Compound Value**    | Work that pays dividends            | People who pay dividends             |
| **High Agency**       | Find ways around obstacles          | Build agency in others               |
| **Force Multiplier**  | Make others more effective          | Make others into multipliers         |
| **Unlocking**         | Remove blockers so others ship      | Teach others to unblock themselves   |
| **Learning Oriented** | Treat projects as skill acquisition | Create learning environments         |
| **Good Questions**    | Know what to ask                    | Teach what to ask                    |
| **Being Helpful**     | Generosity compounds                | Model generosity so they compound it |

## Identifying Successors

Not everyone is ready. Not everyone wants it.

### Signs of Potential

- Asks "why" not just "how"
- Takes ownership beyond their scope
- Already doing the job before having the title
- Seeks feedback, not just validation
- Makes others around them better

### Signs They're Not Ready

- Needs to win every argument
- Blames external factors consistently
- Avoids ambiguity rather than navigating it
- Hoards information or opportunity
- Measures success only by personal output

**The move:** Don't pick the most talented. Pick the most teachable.

## Knowledge Transfer

What you know is worthless if it dies with you.

### Modes of Transfer

| Mode              | When to Use                             | Limitation                |
| ----------------- | --------------------------------------- | ------------------------- |
| **Pairing**       | Complex judgment calls, tacit knowledge | Doesn't scale             |
| **Review**        | Code, designs, decisions                | Reactive, not proactive   |
| **Documentation** | Process, architecture, rationale        | Loses nuance              |
| **Teaching**      | Principles, mental models               | Requires their engagement |
| **Delegation**    | Real stakes, real learning              | Risk of failure           |

### What Transfers Poorly

- Intuition (takes reps)
- Relationships (can't transfer)
- Judgment (you build it, not receive it)
- Taste (learned by exposure, not instruction)

### What Transfers Well

- Mental models and frameworks
- Checklists and heuristics
- War stories with extracted lessons
- Explicit trade-off criteria
- Questions to ask in specific situations

**The discipline:** Write down what you wish someone had told you.

## Being a Sponsor

Sponsorship is putting your capital behind someone else.

### What Sponsors Do

- Advocate in rooms they're not in
- Put them forward for high-visibility work
- Provide air cover when they take risks
- Stake reputation on their success

### How to Sponsor Well

| Action                             | Why It Matters                             |
| ---------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------ |
| Be specific in advocacy            | "They should lead X" beats "they're great" |
| Follow up after opportunities      | Shows you're invested, not performative    |
| Give them the win publicly         | Credit is not zero-sum                     |
| Protect them from unfair criticism | Your capital absorbs hits theirs can't     |
| Tell them you're sponsoring them   | Alignment prevents surprises               |

### The Risk

Sponsorship costs capital. Sponsor wrong and you lose credibility. Sponsor right
and you build a network of capable people who remember.

**The test:** Have you sponsored someone in the last quarter?

## Measuring Success

The Mentor's metrics are lagging indicators.

| Metric                                | What It Shows        |
| ------------------------------------- | -------------------- |
| Promotions of people you've developed | They grew            |
| Times they've taught others           | The lineage extends  |
| Decisions made without you            | They don't need you  |
| Problems solved you didn't know about | They're autonomous   |
| People who credit you years later     | The impact persisted |

### The Paradox

Success means obsolescence. If they still need you, you haven't finished.

## Common Traps

| Trap                         | Pattern                          | Fix                           |
| ---------------------------- | -------------------------------- | ----------------------------- |
| **Mini-me**                  | Developing clones of yourself    | Value different strengths     |
| **Hoarding**                 | Keeping the interesting work     | Give away what you're good at |
| **Rescuing**                 | Stepping in before they struggle | Let them fail safely          |
| **Assuming readiness**       | Promoting before they're ready   | Stretch, don't break          |
| **Neglecting documentation** | Knowledge in your head only      | Write it down now             |
| **Sponsoring favorites**     | Bias toward people like you      | Sponsor across difference     |

## Questions to Ask

### Weekly

- Who did I develop this week?
- What did I delegate that I used to own?
- Whose work did I make visible?

### Quarterly

- Who is closer to ready than last quarter?
- What knowledge have I transferred?
- Who have I sponsored?

### Yearly

- If I left, who would step up?
- What have I documented that will outlast me?
- Is my lineage extending beyond my direct reports?
